Kacchi Biryani

🍖
Main Dish

⏱

3–4 hrs

Cook Time

👥

6–8

Servings

🌶

Medium

Spice

Ingredients

1 kg mutton (bone-in)

500g basmati rice

1 cup full-fat yoghurt

4 large potatoes

2 cups fried onions

2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

½ cup ghee

½ tsp saffron + warm milk

Whole spices (bay, cinnamon, cardamom, clove)

Kewra water, salt to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1
    Marinate Overnight

    Combine mutton with yoghurt, ginger-garlic paste, biryani masala, salt, and half the fried onions. Marinate for at least 4 hours (overnight preferred).

  2. 2
    Fry Potatoes & Parboil Rice

    Fry potatoes golden. Soak basmati rice 30 minutes, then boil in salted water with whole spices until 70% done. Drain well.

  3. 3
    Layer the Pot

    Spread marinated raw mutton at the base, place fried potatoes on top, then layer parboiled rice. Drizzle saffron milk, ghee, kewra, and remaining fried onions.

  4. 4
    Dum Cooking

    Seal the pot tightly with dough or foil. Cook on high for 10 min, then reduce to lowest flame. Dum cook 45–60 minutes until mutton is tender.

  5. 5
    Rest & Serve

    Open seal gently. Rest 10 minutes before serving. Plate carefully keeping layers intact. Serve hot with raita and boiled eggs.

💡 Chef's Tip

Place a tawa (griddle) under the pot during dum to prevent scorching — the secret to a perfectly cooked kacchi every time.

Beef Rezala

🥩

⏱

2 hrs

Cook Time

👥

4–6

Servings

🌿

Mild

Spice

Ingredients

1 kg beef (boneless)

1.5 cups yoghurt

3 tbsp poppy seed paste

2 onions, sliced

4–6 green chillies

½ tsp mace + nutmeg

½ cup ghee

Kewra water, white pepper, salt

Instructions

  1. 1
    Marinate Beef

    Mix beef with yoghurt, poppy seed paste, ginger-garlic, white pepper, and salt. Let rest for at least 2 hours.

  2. 2
    Fry Onions & Add Whole Spices

    Heat ghee in a heavy pot, fry onions until golden. Add cardamom, cloves, bay leaf, and mace. Sauté 1 minute.

  3. 3
    Cook the Beef

    Add marinated beef and cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until beef releases its juices and the gravy thickens, about 45 minutes.

  4. 4
    Simmer & Finish

    Add green chillies and a splash of kewra water. Simmer on low for another 20 minutes until oil floats on top. Serve with naan or rice.

💡 Chef's Tip

Rezala should have a pale, creamy white-to-golden gravy. Avoid using red chillies or turmeric — the colour comes from ghee and yoghurt only.

Chicken Roast

🍗

⏱

1.5 hrs

Cook Time

👥

4

Servings

🌶

Mild-Med

Spice

Ingredients

1 whole chicken (cut into 8)

1 cup yoghurt

2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

1 tsp turmeric

2 tsp roast masala

2 large onions, sliced

3 tbsp oil or ghee

Sugar 1 tsp, salt to taste

Instructions

  1. 1
    Score & Marinate

    Score the chicken pieces deeply. Mix with yoghurt, ginger-garlic, turmeric, roast masala, and salt. Marinate 2–3 hours.

  2. 2
    Fry Onions

    Fry sliced onions in ghee until deep golden-brown. Remove half for garnish, leave half in pot.

  3. 3
    Cook Chicken

    Add marinated chicken to the pot. Cook on medium-high, turning, until the marinade dries and chicken starts to brown, about 25 minutes.

  4. 4
    Finish & Glaze

    Add 1 tsp sugar and a splash of water. Cover and cook 15 min until oil separates and a thick, shiny masala coats the chicken. Garnish with reserved onions.

💡 Chef's Tip

The sugar is key — it gives the chicken roast its signature glossy, slightly caramelised finish that sets the Bangladeshi version apart.

Shemai

🍜

⏱

30 mins

Cook Time

👥

4–6

Servings

🍬

Sweet

Flavour

Ingredients

200g thin shemai vermicelli

1 litre full-fat milk

¾ cup sugar (adjust to taste)

3 tbsp ghee

4 green cardamom pods

2 tbsp raisins

¼ cup cashews + almonds

1 tsp rose water

Instructions

  1. 1
    Toast the Vermicelli

    In a heavy pan, melt 2 tbsp ghee over medium heat. Add shemai and stir continuously until it turns golden brown and fragrant, about 5–7 minutes.

  2. 2
    Fry Nuts & Raisins

    In remaining ghee, fry cashews and raisins until raisins puff and nuts are golden. Set aside for garnish.

  3. 3
    Simmer in Milk

    Add warm milk and cardamom to the toasted shemai. Cook on medium-low, stirring often, until vermicelli is soft and milk thickens, about 15–18 minutes.

  4. 4
    Sweeten & Serve

    Add sugar and rose water, stir and cook 3 more minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature, topped with fried nuts and raisins.

💡 Chef's Tip

Always toast the shemai in ghee before adding milk — this prevents it from becoming mushy and gives the dish its signature nutty aroma.

Firni

🥣

⏱

1hr+chill

Total Time

👥

6

Servings

❄️

Chilled

Served

Ingredients

1 litre full-fat milk

3 tbsp rice flour (or ground raw rice)

¾ cup sugar

A generous pinch of saffron

1 tsp rose water

4 cardamom pods, ground

2 tbsp crushed pistachios

Dried rose petals for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1
    Infuse Saffron

    Warm 2 tbsp of the milk and steep saffron in it for 10 minutes until the milk turns deep golden yellow.

  2. 2
    Make Rice Slurry

    Mix rice flour with ½ cup cold milk until smooth with no lumps. Set aside.

  3. 3
    Cook on Gentle Heat

    Bring remaining milk to a gentle boil with cardamom. Add the rice slurry slowly while stirring constantly. Cook on low heat 20–25 minutes until thick and creamy.

  4. 4
    Set & Chill

    Stir in sugar, saffron milk, and rose water. Pour into individual bowls (earthen pots look stunning). Refrigerate at least 3 hours. Garnish with pistachios and dried rose petals before serving.

💡 Chef's Tip

Never stop stirring while the milk thickens — it prevents scorching and gives firni its signature glass-smooth texture. Serve in clay bowls for authenticity.

Our Story

Ramadan, Eid & This Journey

From morning sweets like Shemai to grand feasts featuring aromatic Biryani and rich Rezala, traditional Eid recipes carry our heritage, keeping family bonds alive across time and borders.
